Scope of Work

The scope of work includes the analysis drone based infrared thermography conducted by the GarudaUAV for NTPC Anantapur (50 MW AC) Solar PV Plant located at Galiveedu (Kadiri). A thorough analysis is done based on the thermal data of 50 MWp (50 MW AC) Solar PV Plant captured.

Objective

Solar companies often struggle to maintain a consistently high-power generation from the installed capacity due to constant recurring issues in solar panels.

The expansive nature of solar farms makes manual inspection of each panel impractical, both in terms of economics and manpower. This process not only consumes significant time and resources but also introduces the risk of human error and biases, ultimately impeding the speed and efficiency of inspection.

Traditional methods of solar panel maintenance are tedious and time consuming where it's difficult to access every panel for inspection. Other major issues in the inspection process are the harsh climatic conditions, unavailability of skilled labor for constant supervision, height of the panels, site design framework and other natural hindrances.

Drone-based thermography identifies issues in solar panels including, but not limited to:

  • a) Identification of hotspots
  • b) Dust over panels
  • c) Identification of shade on panels
  • d) Possibility of PID
  • e) Cell mismatches
  • f) Broken or cracked module
  • g) Module degradation
  • h) Bird dropping
  • i) Vegetation encroachment
  • j) Open circuit
  • k) Bypassing of diodes
  • l) Reverse polarity
  • m) Disconnected String
  • n) Isolated Module

Methodology

The Block was covered in multiple flights using DJI Thermal Camera sensor mounted on a UAV The data collection process is explained in the following steps:

  1. Custom quad-copter drone is loaded with the appropriate flight plans.
  2. The drone is equipped with:
    • 48 MP camera (visual)
    • DJI Radiometric industrial grade thermal camera (09 mm focal length)
  3. The calibration of thermal and visual camera is duly verified.
  4. Before initiating the drone flight, ideal flight path is identified for unencumbered operations.
  5. GarudaUAV's proprietary mobile application helps create an automated flight plan for the site.
  6. Area of interest is precisely identified, and inspection carried out covering the entire area
  7. The images are carefully extracted post inspection.
  8. These images are uploaded to our cloud platform where the inspection team runs automated services to identify anomalies and tag them. These are then compiled into a final report.
